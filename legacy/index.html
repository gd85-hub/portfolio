About

I design interfaces for complex, data-heavy tools, the kind where a user has to read a lot of state and make a real decision. My background is unusual for a designer: about a decade in marketing, research, and project management before UX, so I read a problem from the business and the interface side before proposing anything.

Today I design internal and client-facing products at Axon Trade, a technology provider for the institutional crypto market. Before that I worked across sales analytics and edtech, and earlier led data and customer-facing teams at companies like June Homes and Standuply, with clients including IBM, Intel, Adobe, and Coursera.

Based in Novi Sad. English (C1), French (B1), Russian (native).

Summary

Product designer for complex, data-dense products. A decade in marketing, research, and project management before UX lets me frame the business problem and design the interface that solves it, not just style the screens.

Experience

Axon Trade - Product Designer, Marketing Generalist

Designing internal and client-facing products for a technology provider serving the institutional crypto market. Led all marketing until 2021, then moved fully into product design.

  • Sole product designer across three client platforms and two in-house tools (under NDA), spanning order flow, market data, and internal operations.
  • Made the product suite white-label-ready, which supported the company's 2023 move into licensed solutions.
  • Redesigned the company's marketing site; the rebuild lifted Lighthouse performance from 26 to 91.

Naoma - Product Designer

Interfaces and user flows for an AI-powered sales analytics platform, alongside engineers from Amazon, Yandex, Aviasales, and Delivery Hero.

  • Reframed the product from a call-quality tool into a sales-performance platform after research showed leaders needed team-wide insight, not just call scores; designed the four core surfaces (dashboard, call scoring, insights, AI coaching).
  • Ran a teardown of 60+ competitors to find the positioning gap and shape product strategy.
  • Interface work contributed directly to the first paying customers and initial funding; by December 2024: 16 qualified leads (~$200K potential ARR), 8 pilots analyzing 50K+ calls, and 2 closed deals.

OilCase - UX/UI Designer

Interfaces for educational IT products in the oil and gas industry.

  • Led an audit-driven redesign of OilCase X, a field-management simulator: a UX/UI audit plus participant interviews surfaced four issue classes that set the priorities; the refreshed product helped close the team's first major B2B client.
  • Built a shared UI kit for the whole OilCase ecosystem, cutting design inconsistency and speeding up development.
  • Owned IA and interface for the courses back-office: a classification model drove the architecture, then 50+ screens across two roles (Admin, Author) covering every state and edge case, shipped to engineering as documented spec.

June Homes - Head of Property Listing and Data Department

Led a team of data managers at a tech-driven US apartment-rental service; owned advertising budgets, process, PropTech partnerships, and listing UX.

  • Expanded platform presence from 7 to 25 with no added ad spend.
  • Restructured process and budgeting: 3x leads, 3x paying customers, and 2x lower CPA.

Standuply - UX Researcher, Head of Customer Success (from 2017)

UX research and Customer Success for a stand-up bot for Slack and Microsoft Teams; clients included IBM, Intel, Adobe, Oracle, and Coursera.

  • Built Customer Success from scratch and ran the research that shaped early product direction: 80+ in-depth user interviews worldwide, user churn down 20%.
  • Product Hunt launches drove 500 upvotes in a single day and a 25% lift in registrations.
